# How To Survive Poison

[00:00] Hi, this is David from MinuteEarth. 87 double espressos are enough to kill you; drink them all at once, and all that caffeine will short out your heart and stop it from beating. Yet coffee shops are filled with plenty of not-dead people who have downed hundreds of cups of coffee over their lifetimes. Here’s why: You can think of your tolerance for caffeine – or any substance, really – as a bathtub; that tub holds the amount of that substance that can circulate in your bloodstream safely at a given time. The more of that substance you take in, the more the tub fills up - and the more effects you might feel, like the jitters from a series of double espressos. But your body will keep humming along. That is, until the tub overflows…at that point, the concentration of the substance in your bloodstream gets too high, and you’ll die.

[00:37] Luckily for us caffeine lovers, like real bathtubs, our body’s poison tubs can drain. As your body excretes potential poisons through pee and poop, or chemically converts them into harmless - or even useful - compounds, the level of a substance in the hypothetical tub lowers. Caffeine, for instance, gets metabolized by the liver into a buffet of organic molecules. And it happens fast – the caffeine in one of those double espressos will drain from your tub in a matter of hours. That's why you can drink pretty much as much caffeine as you want, as long as you space it out. Cyanide - surprisingly - also drains quickly, as your body speedily converts it to vitamin B12. So even though your cyanide tub is tiny, eating a few cyanide-packed apple seeds every day is perfectly safe. Lead, on the other hand, leaves the body really slowly because it gets stored in bones. So even though your lead tub isn’t tiny, since pretty much every lead exposure throughout your life slowly inches the level in the tub upwards, it’s more likely to overflow.

[01:26] These tubs exist for pretty much every substance we ingest - even water. If you manage to guzzle more than 6 liters at once, your body’s water tub will overflow; all that water will cause your cells to swell beyond repair. Luckily, that tub empties super fast; not only does your body use water for pretty much every biological process, you can also pee out any extra water within minutes. Now that’s what I call draining the main vein.

LEARN MORE

To learn more about this topic, start your googling with these keywords: 
Poison: A substance that is capable of causing illness or death when introduced or absorbed.
Overdose: A dangerously large dose of a substance.
Biotransformation: The process by which foreign chemicals are transformed by the body into less toxic substances.
Polydipsia: The medical term for feelings of extreme thirst that can lead to hyponatremia.
Hyponatremia: Abnormally low sodium in the blood often caused by excessive water consumption. 
Lethal Concentration - The concentration a substance needs to be in the bloodstream to kill 50% of subjects. 
Metabolism - the chemical processes that occur within a living organism in order to maintain life.
Toxicity - the quality of being toxic or poisonous.
Excretion - the process of eliminating or expelling waste matter.
