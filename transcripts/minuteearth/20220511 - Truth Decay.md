# Truth Decay

[00:00] It’s rare to see a claim on social media that isn’t challenged in replies and comments. It seems like our ability to agree on what’s true is deteriorating. But is that really the case? Well, whether we take a claim to be true or not, often depends on whether we trust the source making that claim. And studies show that despite the increasing availability and consumption of online news sources, more than one third of Americans say that news has become less reliable now than it was in the past. And what’s more, the ingredients that news sources are using to convey information are changing too. Descriptive and retrospective language – like references to numbers, time, and concrete objects – is becoming more conversational, instead emphasizing interpersonal interactions, emotions, and personal perspectives. This blurring of the line between facts and opinions is creating political polarization and paralysis.

[00:52] After all, an opinion is a much more abundant and delicious ingredient to use than a fact is when cooking up enticing content. We like our brain-food to be easily consumable so we can quickly scroll on to the next twenty recommended posts. And just like the over-salted, artificially sweetened foods that are so delicious but nutritionally deficient, this brain-sugar is satisfying our attention without providing much substance and leaving us factually deficient. But wait, let’s pause for a second… What about this video? I just made a bunch of claims without mentioning where I got that information. As long as these claims didn’t run counter to your intuition, then this beautifully blended brain-shake may have gone down so easily that you didn’t even notice. But it’s not always a good thing to swallow ideas without checking the nutrition label first. Keep in mind, both good and bad ideas can be nicely packaged. When you remove the pleasant background music, well-processed audio, and playful illustrations… What's left? And how do you know if it’s actually nutritious for your brain?

## Description

Trust is eroding, in part, due to the over-abundance of opinion-based content; we must all develop better tools and habits for consuming information to regain a shared understanding of what is true.
