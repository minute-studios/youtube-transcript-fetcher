# How We Make MinuteEarth Videos (Behind the Scenes)

[00:00] Hey, this is Henry. I am here to talk about how MinuteEarth videos get made. We're a truly internet-based production company. We have at times spanned eight times zones, so we communicate using tools like Slack, Dropbox and Google Hangouts and a bunch of other things for file sharing and working together in collaborating on creative projects across the world. MinuteEarth videos begin as an idea or pitch from somebody - one of our writers - who's excited about something and want to share it and they tell the rest of us about it and we ask questions and get normally also excited about it they go off and learn more about it and do research and come back with a draft. In the research process writers will either read books or scientific papers to learn about material themselves or they'll talk to researchers to find out what's the newest thing in the field or try to get a better sense of understanding of the subject. It's a very iterative process of revisions, and drafts, and revisions and drafts that sometimes involves conversations between editor and writer, or feedback from the entire team. And then at the end we do a little bit of tiding up of the writing and he goes off the narrator and they narrate the video. Then we send the audio to Ever who does the audio editing and he'll cut the narration down from a bunch of takes, often with mistakes, into something that sounds good and is part of the video. And we must not forget that we also add music to the video as well. Then we go into storyboarding where the illustrator for the video will do kind of rough sketches for what is going to happen in each scene of the video.

[01:28] We will have several iterations of video storyboards, where we're trying to get feedback from everyone, and the writer particularly, but also everyone on the team as to how we can best visually convey the material we're talking about. Only when we have zeroed in where we are going with the storyboard, do we then have the illustrator do the final fancy looking drawings. We invariably have multiple at that stage as well but because the final drawings take more time we try to get as close to the ideal video as possible in the storyboards stage so you don't have to spend as much time doing the illustrations of changing illustrations that we already spent a lot of time doing. Finally once it is done we will post it online on places like Vessel and YouTube so that you can watch it, which is, I assume, where you're watching this video.

[02:08] This might sound like a long and convoluted process but it's made extra complicated by the fact that we're actually doing this in parallel for up to 8 videos at a time some are in stages of writing, some are at stages of storyboarding and illustration, so that we can have videos ready to go at more regular intervals. Hopefully you've enjoyed that little behind the scenes look at how MinuteEarth is made. Have a good one.
